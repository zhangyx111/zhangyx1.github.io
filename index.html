Biography

I am an Associate Professor at Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications (BUPT). I serve as a committee member of the Antennas and RF Society of the China Institute of Communications, a committee member of the Intelligent Metasurface Technology Committee, and a committee member of the Youth Working Committee of the Beijing Institute of Communications. My research focuses on wireless channel measurement, modeling, prediction, and digital-twin-related technologies for mobile communications. I have published 60+ SCI/EI-indexed papers in related areas, including 1 ESI Highly Cited Paper and 3 Hot Papers; hold 10+ authorized invention patents; and was selected for the Beijing Association for Science and Technology Young Talent Support Project and the “6G Rising Star Young Scholar” of the Global 6G Conference.

I have led projects such as the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) Young Scientists Fund and sub-tasks of the National Key R&D Program, and have participated in 10+ national and ministerial-level projects, including NSFC programs, National Key R&D projects, and National Science and Technology Major Projects. My contributions include 30+ proposals adopted by international standardization organizations such as 3GPP and ITU, and I have helped produce 1 item of 3GPP international standards.

I received the Second Prize of the China Institute of Communications Science and Technology Progress Award (first contributor, 2025). As a major contributor, I received the First Prize of the Chinese Institute of Electronics Science and Technology Progress Award (2025), the First Prize of the China Institute of Communications Technology Invention Award (2021), the First Prize of the Chinese Institute of Electronics Electronic Information Teaching Achievement Competition (2025), the Gold Medal of the 49th International Exhibition of Inventions Geneva, and the IEEE ICCC 2025 Best Demonstration Award.

Research Interests

  1. Wireless channel measurement and modeling for complex space–air–ground–sea scenarios toward 6G mobile communications
  2. Foundation models, intelligent agents, and embodied intelligence for wireless channel and network digital twins
  3. Wireless channel emulation and testing methodologies for future mobile communication systems and integrated space–air–ground–sea communication systems
